Demos


Photo 13: Introducing the robot and its design aspects to the DARPA team.


Photo 14: The primary focus of the demo was the operational advantages of a continuum robot arm relative to an articulated arm. Shown here in the foreground is the iRobot Packbot EOD.


Photo 15: Demonstration of speed. The Octarm can make swift flailing movements, a potential crowd disrupter.


Photo 16: The octarm successfully picks up a large drum, a hopeless task for the packbot arm.


Photo 17: The octarm can manipulate shapes that are difficult for a traditional robot arm, such as a ball.


Photo 18: 'Whole Arm Manipulation' enables manipulation of small as well as large objects with equal ease.


Photo 19: Picking up many objects at the same time. A traditional manipulator can usually handle only one object at a time.


Photo 20: The stream crossing obstacle, part of the Small Robotic Vehicles Test Bed (SRVTB) at SWRI.


Photo 21: Demonstration of under-water grasping.


Photo 22: Demonstration of grasp stability.


Photo 23: Toys! Inert German and Russian ammunition, and fake rifles.


Photo 24: Demonstration of Octarm's capabilities in safe disposal of explosive ordinance.


Photo 25: Stacking cones.
